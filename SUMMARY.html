STT End-of-Turn Detection

Submission Summary & Architecture Breakdown

Architecture Breakdown

1. Feature Extraction Engineering

Our strictly causal CPU-only feature extraction acts on the final 1.5-second buffer window immediately preceding a detected pause:

F0 Pitch Trajectory RMS Energy Decay Vowel Elongation Block ZCR/RMS Breath Signature Language Flag

We specifically bounded `librosa.yin` searches to human speech frequencies (65-400Hz) and applied rigorous `NaN` fallbacks for silent or unvoiced gaps to ensure stability and vastly improve processing speed.

2. Model Training & Thresholding

We deployed a HistGradientBoostingClassifier (scikit-learn) configured with balanced class weights to process the causal feature matrices. Instead of applying arbitrary scaling, our `predict.py` directly outputs raw uncalibrated probabilities. This enables the official scoring script to organically sweep all probability bounds without hitting warped density distortions, finding the precise global optimal point.

Human & AI Contribution Split

Human Contribution (Concept & Design)

  • Defining the strict acoustic principles (e.g., Trailing breath vs. vowel elongation).
  • Structuring the YIN boundary conditions, optimization parameters, and finite-value cleaning logic.
  • Directing the architectural requirement for raw probability output to prevent density warping.
  • Defining evaluation criteria for the threshold sweep constraint.

AI Execution (Automation & Syntax)

  • Translating acoustic designs into optimized, vectorized `numpy` and `librosa` code structures.
  • Handling the cross-validation logic (`GroupShuffleSplit`) and building `train_model.py`.
  • Managing OS-level environment hurdles (WSL mapping) and migrating serialization logic from `joblib` to standard `pickle`.
  • Writing boilerplate file management, CLI arguments, and CSV saving flows in `predict.py`.
