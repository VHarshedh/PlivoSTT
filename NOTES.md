Our model relies on a strictly causal 1.5-second acoustic window to extract a 256-dimensional prosodic embedding via `resemblyzer`, fused with hand-crafted signals for Energy Drop, Voicing Density, Turn Duration, and Language. 
To prevent overfitting on our small dataset, we utilize an L1-regularized Logistic Regression to perform aggressive feature selection, distilling the dimensions down to the ~160 most crucial signals. 
These refined features are then piped into a Random Forest Classifier to capture complex, non-linear end-of-turn patterns, ultimately tuned to an optimal threshold of 0.50 and delay of 250ms using 5-Fold GroupKFold CV.
The model still struggles with brief affirmative utterances (e.g., "yeah", "mhm") in Hindi, where the prosody mimics an end-of-turn but the semantic intent is merely a conversational continuation (a "hold"). 
It also occasionally fails when background noise suddenly drops, tricking the Energy Drop feature into predicting a false cutoff.
With one more day, we would expand our causal window testing and implement a causal pitch-tracking algorithm (like pYIN) to explicitly detect pitch-downs at the end of sentences, avoiding the black-box nature of the embeddings. 
We would also build a lightweight, causality-compliant Voice Activity Detector (VAD) to better filter out background noise anomalies.
